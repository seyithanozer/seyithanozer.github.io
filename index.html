Hello! I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the Royal College of Art School of Architecture. I am interested in the methodologies and methods of architectural knowledge and its systematisation. My current research focuses on housing design standards and regulations. I am building this website as a repository for my academic works, to learn front-end development and experiment with web-based narratives.

Özer, Seyithan, and Alasdair Jones. 'Changing Socio-Spatial Definitions of Sufficiency of Home: Evidence from London (UK) before and during the Covid-19 Stay-at-Home Restrictions'. International Journal of Housing Policy, published ahead of print, December 1, 2022. ↗︎

Ozer, Seyithan and Sam Jacoby. 'The design of subsidized housing: towards an interdisciplinary and cross-national research agenda'. Housing Studies, published ahead of print, March 2, 2022. ↗︎

In this article developed as part of the project 'Housing Standardization: Design Guidelines, Regulatory Frameworks, and Policy', we explored how design research can benefit comparative housing studies by studying subsidized housing systems and housing design in 20 countries.

Özer, Seyithan, and Sam Jacoby. 'Dwelling Size and Usability in London: A Study of Floor Plan Data Using Machine Learning'. Building Research & Information 50, no. 6 (2022): 694–708. ↗︎

In this article developed from my PhD, we analysed the size and usability of dwellings in London and quantified the extent to which dwellings fail both recent and historical space standards, discussing the effectiveness of space standards in relation to dwelling usability and issues of design.
