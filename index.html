I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the Royal College of Art School of Architecture. I am interested in the methodologies and methods of architectural knowledge and its systematisation. My current research focuses on housing design standards and regulations.

Özer, Seyithan, and Sam Jacoby. 'Dwelling Size and Usability in London: A Study of Floor Plan Data Using Machine Learning'. Building Research & Information 50, no. 6 (2022): 694–708. ↗︎

In this article developed from my PhD, we analysed the size and usability of dwellings in London and quantified the extent to which dwellings fail both recent and historical space standards, discussing the effectiveness of space standards in relation to dwelling usability and issues of design.
